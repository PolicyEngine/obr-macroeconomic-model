# Calibration scorecard

How well does the **raw** model (add-factor residuals OFF) reproduce the OBR's
published baseline? The anchored model matches by construction, so the raw-vs-OBR
gap is the true measure of calibration. Generated by
`obr_macro/calibration_score.py` (raw model vs EFO, 2025Q1–2027Q4).

**The headline trap:** a `0.00%` error usually does **not** mean a good forecast
— it means the model left the variable at the OBR's published value because its
equation is dead or exogenous (*passthrough*). Only variables the model actually
**computes** measure calibration. And **Employment scores 0.00% as a trivial
identity** (`ETLFS = HWA/AVH`, both passthrough inputs), not as a behavioural win.

| Business investment | 72.19% | computed — **off** |
| Employment | 0.00% | computed — **trivial identity** |

| Household income | 6.61% | computed — fair |
| Real household income | 6.36% | computed — fair |
| Company profits | 31.90% | computed — **off** |

## Honest score
- **11 of 21** headline variables are actually computed; the other **10 are passthrough**.
- Of the 11 computed: 1 is a trivial identity, and the rest run from **fair to off**.
- **8 of 11 land within a usable band (73%)**; business investment, company
  profits and RPI are off.

## Net balances are scored as % of GDP — in *both* scorecards
Current account and trade balance are scored as a share of GDP, not as a % of
their own (tiny) value: a difference of two ~£240bn gross flows is meaninglessly
amplified by a %-of-own-value metric (it read 27% / 68% that way). This is the
OBR's own convention. Crucially it is now applied **consistently in both this
scorecard and the forecast scorecard** (`forecast.py`) — previously it lived only
in the forecast scorecard, which is the kind of selective metric that flatters a
headline. Scored the same way everywhere, both balances are *fair*.

## Verdict
Calibration is **partial and uneven**: fair on GDP shape, consumption, incomes,
unemployment and (as % of GDP) the net balances; a trivial identity on
employment; and **off on business investment, company profits and RPI**. The ~10
dead/exogenous channels (exports, imports, prices, labour demand, wages) are held
at the OBR value.

Note these figures are **worse than earlier versions of this doc** on business
investment and profits. That is deliberate: two correctness fixes removed props
the old numbers rested on — (1) the fiscal-input data was held flat at a single
seasonal quarter, which fortuitously sat near the EFO on some channels, and (2)
several previously-**silently-dead** equations (mixed-case financial-account
block, `CGC`, ratio-lag deflators) are now alive but not yet add-factor
calibrated. The earlier scores were partly an artefact of those two bugs.
Improving these numbers is now honest calibration work on live equations, bounded
by the constants and deflator histories the OBR does not publish.
